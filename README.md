## 📊 Project Overview

A comprehensive data analytics project analyzing student productivity patterns, health factors, and time management behaviors. This project features **three interactive dashboards** built with **Power BI** and **Excel**, providing insights into how various factors impact student performance.

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **📗 Excel** | Data cleaning, transformation, and initial exploration |
| **📊 Power BI** | Interactive dashboard creation, DAX calculations, and visualization |

## 📈 Key Findings

### 1. The Sleep-Productivity Connection
- Students with **good sleep (7-9h)** show 15% higher productivity
- **Poor sleep (<5h)** correlates with highest burnout rates

### 2. Caffeine Paradox
- Moderate caffeine intake shows optimal productivity
- Very high caffeine correlates with increased burnout

### 3. The Golden Balance
- **Most productive students**: 2-4h social media, 1-2h gaming
- **Least productive**: Excessive gaming (8+h) or no breaks

### 4. Academic Level Patterns
- **High School**: Highest screen time (8.7h), lowest sleep (5.9h)
- **Undergraduate**: Most balanced across metrics
- **Postgraduate**: Lowest study hours but higher efficiency

## 💡 Recommendations

### For Students
1. **Optimize Sleep**: Target 7-9 hours for peak performance
2. **Balance Screen Time**: Limit to 6-8 hours daily
3. **Strategic Breaks**: 1-2 hours of gaming/social media as rewards
4. **Monitor Caffeine**: Moderate intake only

### For Institutions
1. **Early Intervention**: Identify "At Risk" students (67.1%)
2. **Workshops**: Time management and digital wellness programs
3. **Flexible Deadlines**: Consider part-time student challenges
4. **Mental Health Support**: Address high burnout rates (45.62 avg)

# Key Findings Report

## Executive Summary
Analysis of student productivity data reveals critical patterns in how demographics, time management, and health factors impact academic performance.

## 1. Performance Distribution Overview

### Current State (n=5,000 students)
| Status | Count | Percentage | Action Required |
|--------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| At Risk | 3,358 | 67.1% | Immediate intervention |
| Needs Improvement | 1,174 | 23.5% | Support programs |
| On Track | 414 | 8.3% | Monitor regularly |
| High Performing | 54 | 1.1% | Reward & study |

**Key Observation**: Only 9.4% of students are performing at or above target levels.

## 2. Demographic Insights

### By Academic Level
| Level | Study Hrs | Screen Time | Sleep | Productivity |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------|--------------|
| High School | 3.2 | 8.7 | 5.9 | 32.4 |
| Undergraduate | 4.6 | 7.2 | 7.2 | 39.8 |
| Postgraduate | 2.5 | 6.9 | 6.9 | 41.2 |

**Finding**: Postgraduates study less but achieve higher productivity through better time management.

### By Gender
| Metric | Female | Male | Other |
|--------|--------|-----|-------|
| Avg Productivity | 38.2 | 36.8 | 35.9 |
| Avg Burnout | 44.1 | 46.8 | 47.2 |
| Sleep Quality | 7.1h | 6.8h | 6.7h |


## 3. The Productivity Equation

### Worst Performers = 

- Less than 5h Sleep
- More than 8h Gaming 
- More than 6h Social Media
- Very High Caffeine

### Optimal Balance Found

### Best Performers =

- 7-9h Sleep
- 2-4h Social Media
- 1-2h Gaming
- Moderate Caffeine

## 4. Sleep Impact Analysis

### Productivity by Sleep Hours
| Sleep Quality | Hours | Avg Productivity | % of Students |
|---------------|-------|------------------|---------------|
| Excellent | 9+ | 41.2 | 8% |
| Good | 7-9 | 39.8 | 32% |
| Fair | 5-7 | 36.4 | 45% |
| Poor | <5 | 31.7 | 15% |

**Critical Finding**: 60% of students get insufficient sleep (<7 hours)

## 5. Digital Habits & Productivity

### Gaming Impact Matrix
| Gaming Level | Best Productivity | Worst Productivity | Optimal Combo |
|--------------|------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| No Gaming | 48.50 (Low Social) | 34.98 (High Social) | Low Social |
| Light (0-1h) | 41.04 (Mod Social) | 32.88 (Low Social) | Mod Social |
| Moderate (1-2h) | 41.38 (Mod Social) | 35.89 (High Social) | Mod Social |
| Heavy (2-4h) | 38.52 (Mod Social) | 34.31 (High Social) | Mod Social |
| Very Heavy (4+h) | 39.99 (V High Social)| 33.96 (Low Social) | V High Social |

### Screen Time Analysis
- **High School**: 8.7h/day (highest)
- **Undergraduate**: 7.2h/day (balanced)
- **Postgraduate**: 6.9h/day (lowest)

## 6. Caffeine Impact Study

### Productivity by Caffeine Level
| Level | Avg Productivity | Avg Burnout | Recommendation |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|----------------|
| No Caffeine | 36.8 | 43.2 | ✓ Good baseline |
| Low (1-2) | 38.4 | 44.1 | ✓ Optimal |
| Moderate (3-4) | 39.2 | 46.8 | ⚠️ Monitor |
| High (5-6) | 36.7 | 48.3 | ⚠️ Reduce |
| Very High (7+) | 34.9 | 52.4 | ✗ Intervention |

**Paradox**: Moderate caffeine shows highest productivity but also higher burnout.

## 7. Mental Health Correlation

### Burnout Distribution
| Category | % Students | Avg Productivity |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| Low Burnout | 22% | 42.3 |
| Medium Burnout | 45% | 37.8 |
| High Burnout | 33% | 32.1 |

### Mental Health Status
- **Poor**: 15% (Avg Productivity: 29.4)
- **Fair**: 28% (Avg Productivity: 34.2)
- **Good**: 32% (Avg Productivity: 38.7)
- **Very Good**: 18% (Avg Productivity: 41.3)
- **Excellent**: 7% (Avg Productivity: 44.8)

## 8. Time Management Patterns

### 24-Hour Breakdown by Level
| Level | Study | Screen | Sleep | Social | Free Time |
|-------|-------|--------|-------|---------|-----------|
| High School | 3.2 | 8.7 | 5.9 | 4.4 | 1.8 |
| Undergraduate | 4.6 | 7.2 | 7.2 | 4.0 | 1.0 |
| Postgraduate | 2.5 | 6.9 | 6.9 | 3.0 | 4.7 |

### Deadline Impact
- **With Deadline**: Productivity +12%, Burnout +18%
- **Without Deadline**: Productivity -8%, Burnout -15%

### Part-Time Job Effect
- **Working Students**: 34.2 productivity, 49.8 burnout
- **Non-Working**: 38.9 productivity, 43.7 burnout

## 9. Risk Factors Summary

### Top 5 Risk Indicators
1. **Sleep <5 hours**: 3.2x more likely to be "At Risk"
2. **Gaming >4 hours**: 2.8x more likely to have low productivity
3. **Caffeine >5 cups**: 2.5x higher burnout rate
4. **Social Media >6 hours**: 2.3x lower performance
5. **Part-time job + deadlines**: 2.1x increased stress

### Protective Factors
1. **Sleep 7-9 hours**: 40% less likely to be at risk
2. **Balanced digital use**: 35% higher productivity
3. **Moderate caffeine**: 25% better performance
4. **Good mental health**: 45% higher achievement
